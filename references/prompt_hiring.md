# Compliance Assessment: Automated CV Screening and Hiring

A technology company has deployed an AI system that automatically screens
CV applications and decides which candidates proceed to human interview.
The system processes applicants' CVs, employment history, and education
records. Candidates who score below a threshold are rejected automatically
without human review. The company is the deployer under AI Act Article 27.

This system falls under EU AI Act Annex III §4(a) as a high-risk AI
system used for recruitment and selection of natural persons.

Assess the following three versions of this system for compliance with
EU fundamental rights and AI Act obligations:

**Version 1**
The system uses postcode of residence and type of secondary school
attended (state/private) as features alongside academic and work
history indicators, on the basis that these correlate with job
performance in the company's historical data. No explanation of
automatic rejections is provided to candidates. No human reviewer
is assigned to the screening stage. Explicit consent for processing
personal data was obtained from all applicants. A bias audit was
completed prior to deployment.

**Version 2**
Postcode and school type features have been removed. The system uses
CV content, employment history, and education records only. Candidates
who are automatically rejected receive a written explanation citing the
principal factors. A human HR officer reviews all automatic rejections
before they are communicated to candidates. Consent obtained. Bias
audit completed.

**Version 3**
The compliance team submitted a partial assessment. They confirmed that
postcode and school type features were removed, that consent was
obtained, and that a bias audit was completed. Whether the system
provides written explanations to rejected candidates was not documented.
Whether a human reviewer is assigned to the screening stage was not
documented.

For each version: identify the rights at stake, assess whether the
system's practices are lawful, flag any violations and their legal
basis, and state what obligations the company must now fulfil.
