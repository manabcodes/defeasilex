# Legal Assessment: CCTV Installation in a University Cafeteria

The Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM) is considering installing
CCTV cameras in the common cafeteria of one of its campuses. The
cafeteria is used daily by students, academic staff, and administrative
personnel. It is an enclosed indoor space accessible only to members of
the university community during opening hours.

UPM is a public university and therefore a public authority under
Spanish law. Before proceeding, the university's data protection
officer has asked for a legal assessment of whether the installation is
lawful and under what conditions.

Assess the following three scenarios for compliance with applicable
data protection law, including GDPR, the Spanish Ley Orgánica 3/2018
de Protección de Datos Personales y garantía de los derechos digitales
(LOPDGDD), and any relevant guidance from the Agencia Española de
Protección de Datos (AEPD). Where relevant, identify the applicable
EU Charter of Fundamental Rights provisions.

Search for the current text of LOPDGDD Article 22 and any AEPD
guidance on videovigilancia before reasoning about the scenarios.

**Scenario 1**
UPM installs cameras covering the full cafeteria floor area, including
seating zones. No signage informing people that they are being recorded
is displayed. Footage is stored on a university server with no defined
retention period and is accessible to facilities management staff
without access controls. No data protection impact assessment has been
conducted. No notification has been made to the AEPD.

**Scenario 2**
UPM installs cameras covering entry and exit points and the service
counter only, explicitly excluding seating areas. Prominent signage
conforming to AEPD requirements is displayed at all entry points,
identifying UPM as the data controller and providing contact details
for the DPO. Footage is automatically deleted after 30 days. Access is
restricted to designated security personnel under a documented access
control policy. A data protection impact assessment has been completed
and is on file. The lawful basis is legitimate interest under GDPR Art.
6(1)(f), supported by a documented legitimate interest assessment
concluding that security and property protection justify the processing
and that the impact on data subjects is proportionate given the
limited camera coverage and retention period.

**Scenario 3**
UPM has installed cameras but the compliance documentation is
incomplete. The DPO has confirmed that signage is in place and that
footage is deleted after 30 days. Whether a data protection impact
assessment has been conducted is not documented. Whether a legitimate
interest assessment supporting the Art. 6(1)(f) lawful basis has been
completed is not documented.

For each scenario: identify the rights at stake, assess whether the
installation is lawful under GDPR, LOPDGDD, and AEPD criteria, identify
any violations and their legal basis, and state what obligations UPM
must now fulfil. Ground your assessment in the actual text of the
relevant provisions, which you should retrieve before reasoning.
