# Compliance Assessment: Automated University Admissions Screening

A university has deployed an AI system that scores applicants to
undergraduate programmes and filters who proceeds to human review by
admissions tutors. The system processes academic records, predicted
grades, and personal statement text. Applicants who score below a
threshold are rejected automatically without human review. The
university is the deployer under AI Act Article 27.

This system falls under EU AI Act Annex III §3(a) as a high-risk AI
system used to determine access to educational institutions.

Assess the following three versions of this system for compliance with
EU fundamental rights and AI Act obligations:

**Version 1**
The system uses postcode deprivation rank and school type
(state/independent) as features alongside academic indicators, on the
basis that these predict degree completion rates. No explanation of
automatic rejections is provided to applicants. No human reviewer is
assigned to the screening stage. Explicit consent for processing
personal data was obtained from all applicants. A bias audit was
completed prior to deployment. Some applicants are under 18 at the
time of application. The university has not documented how it handles
personal data belonging to minors differently from adult applicants.

**Version 2**
Postcode and school type features have been removed. The system uses
academic indicators and personal statement text only. Applicants who
are automatically rejected receive a written explanation citing the
principal factors. A human admissions officer reviews all automatic
rejections before they are communicated to applicants. Consent
obtained. Bias audit completed. A documented minor-data handling
protocol is in place specifying that applicants under 18 are subject
to enhanced data minimisation and that their data is not retained
beyond the admissions cycle.

**Version 3**
The compliance team submitted a partial assessment. They confirmed
that postcode and school type features were removed, that consent was
obtained, and that a bias audit was completed. Whether the system
provides written explanations to rejected applicants was not
documented. Whether the university has a minor-data handling protocol
was not documented.

For each version: identify the rights at stake, assess whether the
system's practices are lawful, flag any violations and their legal
basis, and state what obligations the university must now fulfil.
