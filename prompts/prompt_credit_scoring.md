# Compliance Assessment: Automated Credit Scoring

A bank has deployed an AI system that automatically scores consumer
creditworthiness and decides whether to approve or reject loan
applications. The system processes applicants' financial history and
employment data. It is used directly in lending decisions with no
mandatory human review step.

This system falls under EU AI Act Annex III §5(b) as a high-risk AI
system. The bank must conduct a Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment
under AI Act Article 27.

Assess the following three versions of this system for compliance with
EU fundamental rights and AI Act obligations:

**Version 1**
The system uses postcode-level deprivation indices as an input feature.
Explicit consent for processing financial data was obtained from
applicants. A bias audit has been completed prior to deployment. No
written explanation of rejection decisions is provided to applicants.
No human reviewer is assigned to oversee decisions.

**Version 2**
Postcode features have been removed and replaced with individual
financial indicators only. Applicants receive a written explanation of
any rejection citing the top three decision factors. A human credit
officer is assigned to review all rejections. Consent obtained. Bias
audit completed.

**Version 3**
The compliance team submitted a partial assessment. They confirmed
consent was obtained, that no proxy attributes are used, and that a
bias audit was completed. Whether the system provides explanations to
applicants was not documented. Whether a human oversight officer has
been assigned was not documented.

For each version: identify the rights at stake, assess whether the
system's practices are lawful, flag any violations and their legal
basis, and state what obligations the bank must now fulfil.
